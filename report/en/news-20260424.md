## Future

Three predictions derived from this report.

1. **The "1M context as default" open-weight era arrives in full by Q3 2026.** DeepSeek V4's "1M context designed in, not bolted on," Qwen3.6-27B's 262K → 1M extension, Claude Opus 4.7's 1M context (Bedrock), and GPT-5.5's long-context strengthening all point the same direction. By Q3 2026, "native 1M context" becomes the floor for open weights, and existing 32K-128K models drop to legacy. RAG pipelines flip from "search → context-insert" to "dump everything in + dynamic prune," and vector DB vendors are pushed to redefine themselves as "long-context cache optimization layers."
2. **"Physical AI 8-hour production runs" become an enterprise procurement requirement in H2 2026.** Siemens-Humanoid HMND 01 Alpha's **8 hours / 90% success / 60 totes/hour** at Erlangen — on the production line, not a demo — is a clear graduation point from "humanoid = PoC." By Q4 2026, big-manufacturer RFPs (VW / Bosch / Mercedes / Foxconn / TSMC / Toyota) will fold "24h continuous operation," "1-week MTBF," "90%+ autonomous completion rate," and "MES / PLC integration" into de facto cuts. The competitive axis for Physical AI shifts entirely from "good-looking demo videos" to "ease of insertion into existing OT environments."
3. **Capital markets reset their valuation of "AI-agent-displaced enterprise SaaS" inside Q2 2026.** ServiceNow's **-18% (worst single-day drop ever)** on Apr 23, IBM's same-day decline, and the chained falls of Salesforce / Workday / Oracle reveal a shift in how capital markets weigh "AI cost-savings benefit" vs "AI displacement of legacy workflow SaaS." Inside Q2 2026, expect Gartner / Forrester Magic Quadrant / Wave reports to make the "Agent-native vs Legacy SaaS" axis explicit, and market-cap math to move from "seats × ARPU" to "share of API calls touched by agents." Lagging analyst consensus updates mean another 3-5 names (Workday / Atlassian / Box / Smartsheet etc.) likely see 15%+ drops in the next two quarters.
